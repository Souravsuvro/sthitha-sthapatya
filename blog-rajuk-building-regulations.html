Understanding RAJUK Building Regulations: What Every Homeowner Must Know

Team Editorial · Dec 10, 2025 · 11 min read

Share:FacebookXLinkedIn

RAJUK (Rajdhani Unnayan Kartripakkha) governs building development in Dhaka and many surrounding areas. Navigating FAR, setbacks, height limits, and approval timelines can be confusing. This guide explains the basics so you know what to expect and why your architect’s choices matter.

FAR (Floor Area Ratio)

FAR determines how much built-up area you can have on your plot. It is expressed as a ratio (e.g. 2.5 or 3.0) and varies by zone and road width. Your architect will calculate the maximum permissible floor area and design within it. Exceeding FAR can lead to rejection or penalties.

Setbacks and Height

Setback rules require open space around the building — front, rear, and sides — for light, ventilation, and fire access. Height restrictions depend on location and road width. We design to maximise usable space while staying fully compliant.

Approval Process and Timelines

Submitting drawings, documents, and fees to RAJUK follows a defined process. Timelines can vary; having complete, correct submissions and an experienced team helps avoid delays. We prepare and submit on your behalf and keep you updated until approval is granted.
