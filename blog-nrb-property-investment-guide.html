NRB Property Investment: Your Complete Bangladesh Guide for 2026

Ar. Rahul Chakraborty · Jan 20, 2026 · 12 min read

Share:FacebookXLinkedIn

Non-Resident Bangladeshis (NRBs) often wish to invest in property back home — for family use, retirement, or rental income. Navigating legal requirements, financing, RAJUK regulations, and land verification from abroad can be complex. This guide outlines the key steps and how an experienced architecture and project management firm can support you.

Legal and Ownership Rules

NRBs can buy and hold property in Bangladesh subject to certain conditions. Ownership structures, tax implications, and inheritance should be clarified with a lawyer. We work with clients and their legal advisors to ensure design and construction align with ownership and compliance.

Land Verification and Due Diligence

Before design or construction, land title, mutation, and encumbrances must be verified. We recommend professional land verification and, where needed, survey and soil testing. Our team can coordinate these steps and align the design with site realities.

RAJUK and Building Approvals

Building permits and RAJUK compliance are mandatory in designated areas. We prepare drawings and submissions and guide you through timelines and requirements. For NRB clients we provide clear updates and remote coordination so you stay informed at every stage.

Financing and Project Management

NRB financing options and payment schedules vary. We structure our services to suit remote clients: phased deliverables, transparent costing, and regular reporting. From concept to handover, we aim to be your single point of contact for design and construction management.
