How to Hire an Architect: A Complete Guide for Bangladeshi Homeowners

Team Editorial · Feb 15, 2026 · 10 min read

Share: Facebook X (Twitter) LinkedIn

Hiring the right architect can make the difference between a smooth, rewarding build and a costly, stressful one. In Bangladesh, where RAJUK approvals, local construction practice, and budget realities all play a role, knowing what to look for is essential. This guide walks you through checking credentials, reviewing portfolios, understanding fee structures, and navigating regulatory requirements so you can choose a firm that fits your project and your expectations.

Check Credentials and Registration

Verify that your architect or firm is registered with the relevant professional body and holds valid practice credentials. Ask for proof of registration and, where applicable, RAJUK-approved project experience. A reputable practice will be transparent about qualifications and past approvals.

Review Portfolios and References

Look at completed projects similar in scale and type to yours — residential, commercial, or NRB-focused. Ask for client references and, if possible, visit a completed building. Portfolios and testimonials give you a sense of design quality, delivery discipline, and client satisfaction.

Understand Fee Structures

Architectural fees may be quoted as a percentage of construction cost, a fixed fee, or a combination. Clarify what is included: concept design, working drawings, RAJUK submissions, site supervision, and interior design. Get a written scope and fee agreement before starting.

RAJUK and Approvals

Ensure your architect has experience with RAJUK submissions, FAR calculations, and local building codes. They should be able to guide you on timelines, required documents, and how design choices affect approval. For NRB clients, confirm they can coordinate remotely and keep the process clear.
